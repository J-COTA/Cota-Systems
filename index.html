About Me

My name is Joe Cota and I have been a professional in the IT industry since 2005. My passion for technology started in the era of dial-up BBSs, which I like to describe as the original form of social media. Today I focus on connecting people with technology by mentoring teams and simplifying complex technical concepts. My current role is a part-time Adjunct Information of Technology Professor at Cerro Coso Community College, which I find most extremely rewarding.

My core strength lies in 'getting my hands dirty' with high stakes projects from M365 tenant migrations (including GCC High) to large scale Azure deployments and server upgrades. I am results driven with a focus on designing, securing, and optimizing on-prem, hybrid and cloud native infrastructures for environments ranging from the small to mid-size organization to DoD and global enterprises.

I have developed deep expertise in navigating security frameworks including RMF (JSTIG’s RMF), ISO (90001, 20000-1, 27001), SOC, HIPAA, and NIST. I am highly experienced in managing the full lifecycle of compliance from creating artifacts and mitigation strategies to managing Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M) to secure critical data for audit and QA compliance departments.

My career also expands to managing Azure tenants for the largest FQHC in the US, global Active Directory forests for Fox Film and Fox Network Groups, and overseeing large-scale VMware deployments for the US Navy. I have also provided robust and secure networks for several ISPs. In these roles I have led cross functional teams of engineers, project managers, developers, and testers to ensure seamless production rollouts.

More recently, I have contributed to and supported CI/CD workflows, utilizing GitHub and Jenkins to automate infrastructure via Terraform. My goal has always been to deliver scalable, highly available solutions that align technical strategy with organizational security and budgetary goals.
